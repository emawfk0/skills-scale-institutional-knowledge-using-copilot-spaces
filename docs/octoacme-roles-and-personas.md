# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Onboarding Coordinator

### Role Summary
Onboarding Coordinators ensure new team members have a smooth, well-structured introduction to OctoAcme's processes, tools, and culture. They design and maintain onboarding programs that accelerate time-to-productivity and foster team integration.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain onboarding checklists and welcome materials
- Schedule orientation sessions and introductions with key stakeholders
- Track onboarding progress and gather feedback from new hires
- Coordinate with Project Managers to assign initial tasks and mentors
- Maintain onboarding documentation and knowledge base
- Identify and address gaps in the onboarding experience

### Goals
- Reduce time-to-productivity for new team members
- Ensure consistent, high-quality onboarding experiences
- Build confidence and team connection from day one
- Continuously improve the onboarding process based on feedback

### Typical Communication
- Welcome emails and onboarding schedules
- Weekly check-ins with new team members (first 30 days)
- Onboarding feedback surveys and retrospectives
- Collaboration with HR and team leads on onboarding improvements

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate first project assignments and mentor pairings
- **Developers**: Facilitate technical onboarding and tool setup
- **Product Managers**: Provide context on product vision and roadmap
- **Documentation Steward**: Ensure onboarding materials are current and accessible

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons bridge the gap between project teams and external or executive stakeholders. They translate technical progress into business value, manage expectations, and ensure timely communication of project status and risks.

### Responsibilities
- Serve as primary point of contact for stakeholder communications
- Translate technical updates into business-focused status reports
- Schedule and facilitate stakeholder review meetings
- Gather stakeholder feedback and requirements
- Escalate critical decisions and blockers to appropriate stakeholders
- Maintain stakeholder register and communication cadence

### Goals
- Keep stakeholders informed and aligned with project progress
- Minimize surprises and manage expectations proactively
- Ensure stakeholder feedback is captured and acted upon
- Build trust through transparent, timely communication

### Typical Communication
- Weekly or bi-weekly stakeholder status updates
- Executive summaries and dashboards
- Escalation notifications for critical issues
- Stakeholder meeting facilitation and follow-ups

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Collaborate on status reporting and risk escalation
- **Product Managers**: Align on product vision and stakeholder priorities
- **Developers**: Gather technical context for stakeholder communications
- **Process Improvement Champion**: Share stakeholder feedback for process improvements

---

## Process Improvement Champion

### Role Summary
Process Improvement Champions drive continuous improvement of project management practices, tools, and workflows. They identify inefficiencies, experiment with solutions, and advocate for changes that enhance team productivity and quality.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze current processes to identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies
- Propose and pilot process improvements
- Facilitate retrospectives and capture action items
- Track improvement metrics and measure impact
- Share best practices and learnings across teams
- Maintain process improvement log and documentation

### Goals
- Eliminate waste and reduce cycle time
- Increase team satisfaction and productivity
- Foster a culture of continuous learning and experimentation
- Scale successful improvements across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking
- Process improvement proposals and results
- Best practice sharing sessions
- Metrics dashboards and improvement reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partner on process optimization and retrospective insights
- **Developers**: Gather feedback on developer experience and tooling
- **Stakeholder Liaison**: Incorporate stakeholder feedback into improvements
- **Documentation Steward**: Ensure process changes are documented and accessible

---

## Documentation Steward

### Role Summary
Documentation Stewards ensure that project and institutional knowledge is captured, organized, and accessible to all team members. They maintain documentation standards, reduce knowledge silos, and enable efficient information discovery.

### Responsibilities
- Establish and maintain documentation standards and templates
- Audit documentation for accuracy, completeness, and accessibility
- Organize and structure knowledge repositories
- Train team members on documentation best practices
- Identify and close documentation gaps
- Integrate documentation into project workflows

### Goals
- Create a single source of truth for project and process knowledge
- Reduce time spent searching for information
- Enable effective knowledge transfer and onboarding
- Maintain living documentation that evolves with the organization

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review requests and feedback
- Knowledge base updates and announcements
- Documentation workshops and training sessions
- Collaboration with authors to improve content quality

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Onboarding Coordinator**: Maintain current onboarding materials and guides
- **Project Managers**: Ensure project artifacts are documented and accessible
- **Process Improvement Champion**: Document process changes and improvements
- **Developers**: Collaborate on technical documentation and code comments
